Roles: UX researcher / UI designer

Background:

(05/20/2020) The Covid- 19 global pandemic has changed the way people around the world work and interact. With no vaccination insight, people must wear PPE (personal protection equipment), Maintain six feet distance, and sanitize their hands after touching outside surfaces.

The Problem:üò∑

Colleges have moved to a predominantly online format. Students are allowed to choose whether they would like to return to campus with restrictions.

For these students returning to campus, Covid-19 has also significantly impacted their experience and made it impossible to navigate campus in the way they did before.

Opportunity Statement:‚ú®

  1. How do students/ faculty feel currently about safety protocols, campus guides to returning to campus?
  2. How can we make it easier for students to come back to campus safely?

Research Stage:

Research Methods:üîé

This project started when mandatory quarantines became the standard practice around the United States.

This research aimed to get a quick and easy sense of how students, professors, and staff think and perceive to be true about the University of Miami's Covi-19 safety protocols.

  • - 1:1 interviews
  • - Secondary research
  • - Competitive research

Persona

During the research process, I created personas to help myself anchor the interviewees' thoughts into a clear end-user. This anchoring helped me focus on my design.





Key insights:üí°

  • Most participants were unaware of the location density feature ‚Äî some were delighted to find out about it but some indicated that even though it's a nice feature, they would not use it because they tend to avoid clustered and confined spaces as much as possible
  • Participants have expressed concerns regarding operation, credibility and exposure on testing and tracing of COVID-19
  • Students would like better indication on whether a space is exceeding the recommended capacity (40/50 still green)
  • Students would like to see all other buildings / common spaces listed in the feature
  • The max capacity value is confusing

Prototype/Design:üéØüé®

During the prototyping phase, I used to research and Web Content Accessibility Guidelines AAA standards to decide on features and design prototypes. I included four main features and six user tasks that Users can complete during the application. Below I will also show some of the design systems work.

Prorotype

Features List

  • PPE/Site testing route Allows students to locate PPE, and testing site from anywhere on campus
  • Self reporting/ Mychat integration Allows students to take a daily assessment of how they feel, use diagnosis of medical professional to generate QR code to allow access to campus
  • AR distance checker Allows students to check their current distance from any given person while on campus
  • Push Notification Push Notifications for daily symptom checker, and distance calculator using cellular bluetooth


App Wireframes

UserTask#1:

Search for PPE/sanitation stations. ü•Ω


Based on the University of Miami's decision to place PPE stations around the campuses, I have added a feature to allow students to locate these stations quickly from anywhere on campus.

The responses from student interviewees influenced this feature. Students did not know where to find the PPE locations.

UserTask#2 :

Select your campus. üè´


The University of Miami has three different campuses the Coral Gables campus, the Marine Biology campus, and the Medical Campus.

Allowing users to select between campuses give them the necessary information about PPE/Sanitation stations, building capacity, and testing site locations related to their campus of choice.

UserTask#3:

Submit a positive/negative test.‚ûï‚ûñ

To ensure a safe return to campus, all people coming on to campus must complete a symptom checker survey.

By incorporating this feature into the "Safeguard" app, users will have one location for all safety-related tasks for their time on campus.

UserTask#4:

Change notification settings. üîî

Giving users a choice to turn off notification settings allows users to interact with the application on their terms, ultimately increasing user enjoyment of the app and is standard practice for applications.

UserTask#5:

Search for a testing site near me. üß™

Like the PPE/ Sanitation station location feature, the testing site locator feature allows users to find an on-campus testing site.

UserTask#6:

Check current distance from other people using AR. üìè

Based on research and interviews, I have included an AR distance checking feature in the application. Users said during interviews that they did not trust the floor makers set up by the university. This feature will allow users to take their safety into their hands by checking their current distance from others for themself.
