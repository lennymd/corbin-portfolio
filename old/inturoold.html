INTURO

A "good education" for students starts with teachers

Roles: UX researcher / UI designer

Background:

Teacher turnover has increased substantially over the past two decades: 5.1% of public school teachers left the workforce in 1992, while 8.4% left in 2005. This 3% increase amounts to about 90,000 additional teachers needing to be hired across the U.S. each year.

The Problem:ü§î

Teachers are most likely to quit in their first few years, a study by the US Department of Education‚Äôs National Center for Education Statistics (NCES) found that 17 percent of new teachers leave their jobs within the first five years of teaching. What makes such statistics troubling is the evidence that high teacher turnover negatively affects student achievement.

Turnover rates are shown to be higher in schools with more students of color and students from low-income families. This also disproportionately impacts students who are English language learners.

During the prototyping phase, I used findings from research and Web Content Accessibility Guidelines AAA standards to decide on features and design prototypes. I decided to include three features to help with encouraging teacher reflection, community building, lesson plan creation, and knowledge sharing.

Prorotype

    Features List

  • Lesson plan creation and review
  • A reflective chatbot feature with the ability to review previous chats
  • Shareability of lessons and chats with other teachers for critique and assistance

Feature#1:

Lesson plan creation and review


Teacher coaches in "Teach for America" interviewed said that new teachers struggled most with creating lesson plans engaging and hitting all the necessary points to create an effective classroom experience.

So I designed a simple lesson plan template that requires teachers to write out the specific aspects of their lesson. This template takes the mental load off of teachers when creating lessons and allows them to spend more time being creative with the content of the lessons.

Feature#2 :

A reflective chatbot feature with the ability to review previous chats


Based on my conversations with educators and educator coaches, I found that being reflective of your teaching helps make a teacher better. After learning this, I decided that including a place for teachers to have an interactive virtual "coaching" conversation would be helpful. I designed a basic chatbot that focuses on open-ended questions allowing users to talk through their day and review what went well and what did not.

Feature#3:

Shareability of lessons and chats

Teachers being able to share their thoughts and plans with others in their profession is one of many ways to gain knowledge and "tips and tricks" necessary to be successful in their career.

So being able to share their reflections, lesson plans, and ideas seemed to be a no-brainer based on the research and interviews.
